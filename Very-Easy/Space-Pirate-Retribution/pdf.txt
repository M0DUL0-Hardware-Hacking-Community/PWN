Brute forcing these leaked addresses, we notice we only get PIE addresses and not libc. It is more
than enough to perform a ret2libc attack due to the second bug here.
Buffer Overflow read(0,&local_58,0x84); because local_58 is 40 bytes long and read reads up
to 0x84 bytes.
With some fuzzing and testing, we see that with seven characters, we get a valid PIE address. After
that, we proceed with a classic ret2libc attack.
